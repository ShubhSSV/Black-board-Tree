Plant Information


Plant Name: Black-board-Tree


Benefits: The Black Board Tree offers numerous medicinal and ecological benefits. Known for its potent medicinal properties in Ayurvedic and traditional medicine, the bark of the Black Board Tree is often used to treat ailments like fever, malaria, respiratory infections, and digestive disorders due to its anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antimalarial properties. The tree's wood is lightweight and durable, making it popular in crafting blackboards, furniture, and paper pulp. Additionally, its abundant foliage purifies the air, making it valuable for urban landscaping as it helps improve air quality.



Season: The Black Board Tree is typically an evergreen, although it may lose leaves in colder, drier climates. It flowers from September to November, producing fragrant, greenish-white flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. The tree also bears fruits during the winter, generally around December to February. Due to its adaptability, the Black Board Tree thrives year-round in regions with suitable tropical or subtropical climates.



Region: Native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, the Black Board Tree is found in countries such as India, Sri Lanka, Thailand, and parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. It grows well in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring warm temperatures and moderate rainfall. The tree can be commonly found along roadsides, in urban parks, and in rural areas across South Asia, where it contributes significantly to local biodiversity and traditional medicine.



Age: The Black Board Tree is a hardy, long-lived species, with a lifespan that can exceed 100 years under favorable conditions. It grows relatively quickly in its early years, reaching substantial heights of up to 20-30 meters when mature. Its longevity and resilience make it a popular choice for plantations and reforestation projects, as it provides enduring shade and ecological benefits for decades.
